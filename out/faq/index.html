Logo Design & Branding FAQ

Real answers. No fluff. Everything you need to know before hiring.

How do I hire a freelance logo designer on Fiverr?

+
The process is simple: Browse our curated list of top-rated designers, check their portfolios to ensure their style matches your vision, and select a package. Once you place an order, you will provide a brief about your business, and the designer starts working immediately.

What information do I need to provide?

+
To get a professional brand identity that fits your business, you should be ready to share: Your business name and slogan, your preferred color palette, examples of logos you like for style reference, and a brief description of your target audience.

Will I get files I can actually use?

+
Yes. While Starter tiers usually provide web-ready PNG and JPG files, we highly recommend tiers that include Vector Source Files (AI, EPS, or SVG). These are essential for printing on business cards, signage, or merchandise without losing quality.

What if I do not like the first design?

+
Most professional designers offer multiple revisions. We specifically feature designers with high revision counts so you can tweak the colors, fonts, and layout until you are 100% satisfied with your custom logo design.

Why are some logos €23 while others are €200+?

+
Pricing typically reflects the designer experience level and the complexity of the package. Basic Tier: Perfect for new startups or side projects needing a quick, high-quality visual. Premium Tier: Usually includes a full professional brand identity (social media kits, stationery design, and multiple concepts).

Are there any hidden fees?

+
No. The price you see on the gig page is what you pay. Fiverr handles the payment securely and only releases the funds to the designer once you have approved the final delivery.

Can I use my new logo on social media immediately?

+
Absolutely. All designers provide high-resolution files optimized for Instagram, Pinterest, and Facebook profile pictures. If you need specific sizes for banners, look for designers who offer a Social Media Kit in their package.
